Mission

Our mission is to promote social inclusion among marginalized persons who are subject to coarse or subtle forms of social exclusion. Entaxis aims to dispute social exclusion and advocate for an inclusive society where diversity is seen as a source of strength and not as a divider.

The aim of the Association is to improve the quality of life of vulnerable and special groups and to promote the human rights of these groups as set out in the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union (2012/C 326/02) and in the national action plan of the Ministry of Transparency and Human Rights (Ministry of Justice, 2014).

Also, the Association aims at the social integration of these groups by creating the appropriate conditions for their equal participation in social and economic life. Activities include promoting the right to education, the right to work, non-discrimination, equality between women and men, the right to social assistance, the rights of the child, and the right of people with disabilities to benefit from measures that will ensure their autonomy, social and professional integration and participation in Community life.

Actions

ERASMUS + PROJECTS: Youth educational training activities for persons aged 15 to 30 years old.

RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT: Action research for informed decision-making, research initiatives for project development, and youth projects participating in EU calls including CERV, HORIZON, etc. programmes.

History

Entaxis has been active as an informal group of youth since 2016 (4youth). With local, national, and international presence and a strong capacity in youth social development activities, Entaxis has been developed into a local Civic Society Organisation (CSO) with the aim to empower vulnerable and marginalized groups in the region of Kavala.

Members of the team are mainly youth from the region of Kavala, including professionals from education, special education, social & political studies, science & technology studies, health sciences, and university volunteers. They have a strong network with the local community and are actively involved in local cultural and social initiatives.

Many members have dynamic local and international experience in youth non-formal educational activities with involvement in Erasmus+ trainings and seminars as participants as well as trainers. The team includes youth with fewer opportunities such as persons with special needs, second-generation immigrants, and unemployed youth.
